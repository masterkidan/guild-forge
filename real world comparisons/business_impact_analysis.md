## 3. End-to-End Workflow: The "Idea to Cash" Cycle

How a business request flows through the framework:

### Phase 1: Strategy & Funding (The Edict)
*   **Input**: CEO sets Q3 Goal: "Launch Mobile Wallet".
*   **Action**: **The Grandmaster** allocates "Mana" (Budget) to the *Chapter of Mobile* and creates the high-level Campaign (Epic).
*   **Business Value**: Instant alignment of capital to strategy.

### Phase 2: Planning & Allocation (The Draft)
*   **Input**: Chapter Masters breakdown features.
*   **Action**:
    *   **The Herald** warns: "Chapter Mobile is understaffed. Borrow 2 Engineers from Web."
    *   **The Quartermaster** generates the Delivery Timeline (Gantt) with 80% confidence interval.
*   **Business Value**: Realistic promises. Finance implies dates based on *data*, not *hope*.

### Phase 3: Execution & Coordination (The Quest)
*   **Input**: Sprints begin.
*   **Action**:
    *   **The Emissary** negotiates API contracts between Mobile and Backend.
    *   **The Sentinel** blocks "sloppy code" (Tech Debt) to prevent long-term liability.
*   **Business Value**: High quality throughput. Prevention of "Integration Hell" at the end of the quarter.

### Phase 4: Delivery & Learning (The Release)
*   **Input**: Feature goes live.
*   **Action**:
    *   **The Ranger** watches production metrics (Success Rate).
    *   **The Investigator** (post-launch) analyzes: "We were 3 days late because of Code Review delays."
*   **Business Value**: Continuous improvement loop. Learning is institutionalized, not lost.
